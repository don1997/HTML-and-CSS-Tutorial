Socrates

"The unexamined life is not worth living."

  • Scholar, teacher, and philosopher in ancient Greece
  • Born around 470 BC.
  • Born in Alopeke and belonged to the tribe Anitochis.
  • Married Xanthippe in his 50s.
  • Had three sons named Lemprocles, Sophroniscus, and Menexenus.
  • Credited as being the first moral philosopher.
  • Most of what we know of him comes from the writings of Plato and Xenephon.
  • Was a hoplite in the Peloponnesian war.
  • Believed that philosophy should achieve practical results for the greater well-bing of society.
  • Developed the Socratic Method.
  • Plato and Xenaphon were students of Socrates.
  • In 399 B.C. Socrates was accused of corrupting the youth, impiety, and heresy.
  • He was setenced to death and drank a mixture of poision hemlock.
